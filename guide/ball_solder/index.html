Kit contents

  • 9 Panels with 7 edge segment PCBs each (needed: 60, spare: 3)
  • 1 Panel with 30 relay PCBs and 30 power distribution PCBs for on the vertices (needed: 29+29, spare: 1+1)
  • 1 Data line connector PCB and 1 power distribution PCB for the top vertex
  • 2 slotted PCBs for connecting the ball to the controller
  • 1 Controller board
  • 1 8 pin header
  • 1 LED panel tester
  • 1 Jig bottom plate
  • 1 Panel with 2 segment curve guides (jig)
  • 1 Panel with 8 slotted PCBs (jig)

Jig

Use either solder...

Or hot glue, which has the advantage of clean disassembly when no longer needed.

A bit of tape can keep the structure from sliding out of the jig too easily.

LED test & de-panelize

Please use a steady USB power supply. The LEDs + tester seem to draw too little current for some USB power banks.

Relay PCBs

Main ball

One side of the edge segment PCB is marked with triangles. Also note that at the bottom, there's a bigger and a smaller tab.

The sides with triangles should go in the sharp corners. (These sides will form the inside of the triangles.)

The sides forming the obtuse angles should be clear of triangles.

Make sure there's no gap between the edge segments and the jig:

Using tape can help.

With the triangle symbols in the sharp corners, the small and large tabs will line up with the right slots in the connector PCB.

Soldering the outward facing PCB is easier without the jig.

Solder all the connecting PCBs except for one vertex.

Slide the two remaining slotted PCBs over the top connector PCBs.

Controller

It's easier to solder the controller to the ball if the header is a bit off-angle.

Make sure the + and - signs on the controller and on the ball line up.

Tack the controller to the ball with one pin first.

So that you can correct the angle of the controller board relative to the ball.

Done

Inspect all solder joints one last time before powering it on.
